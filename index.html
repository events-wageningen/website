Freedom through the lenses of gender and sexuality

Freedom Tours Wageningen offers a fresh and deeply personal exploration of freedom and its connection to gender and sexuality in the City of Liberation. During this two-hour interactive tour in downtown Wageningen, tour participants walk with local guides and explore freedom through the lenses of gender and sexuality. Each stop along the tour offers an opportunity for dialogue and reflection, not just observation. Guides share personal stories and invite participants to do the same, creating a space for open conversation and connection across differences. This new responsible tourism initiative invites both residents and visitors to see Wageningen through new eyes. Unlike traditional tours, Freedom Tours Wageningen has been co-created by local residents over the course of several months as they learned from each other and from their communities. Together, they developed an interactive route that reflects the lived experiences, social concerns, and hopes of the people who call this city home. Free monthly tours will be offered from September 2025 to June 2026. This tour (in English) is free, but registration is required. Sign up via the link: freedomtourswageningen.nl After registration the start location will be shared with you.

Downtown Wageningen, Wageningen

Shuteen Erdenebaatar Quartet Jazz Concert

Rising star artist of the year Shuteen Erdenebaatar, een groot jazztalent wereldwijd De in 1998 in de Mongoolse hoofdstad Ulaanbaatar geboren Erdenebaatar is een van de sensaties van haar generatie! Na haar klassieke studie in Mongolië bracht haar liefde voor jazz haar naar Duitsland. Hier studeerde ze jazzpiano en jazzcomposer. Al snel, en dat is best bijzonder voor pianisten, ontwikkelde ze haar eigen stem. Deze stem vormt een uniek geluid met haar verlangende melodieën, verrijkt met diepe en expressieve harmonieën en ritmes. Haar klassieke basis is overal onmiskenbaar aanwezig, wat duidelijk blijkt uit haar technische briljantie, stilistische finesse en de thematische structuur van haar composities. Na haar debuutalbum 'Rising Sun', dat in september 2023 uitkwam op het gerenommeerde label Motéma Music uit New York, werd ze al snel erkend en gevierd als een van de meest veelbelovende rijzende sterren van de Europese en wereldwijde jazzscene. Dit album met haar kwartet zorgde voor een golf van sensatie in de pers en bij het publiek! Hoe blij zijn we dan ook dat we haar bij Jazz in Wageningen mogen begroeten!

Kick-off event for Critical Alternative Research Methods Course

How does the question shape the answer? How to make sense of erased, obscured or silenced data? In this event, we invite a panel of journalists and Intelligence Studies scholars to explore the distinct, but often intersecting, paths of the journalist and the academic investigation in quest of social justice. In his remarkable piece of work, the documentary "Why Srebrenica had to fall”, journalist Huub Jaspers exposes the hidden political failures underlying Bosnia mass atrocities of the 1990s. The public will be invited to compare, contrast, and critique different ways of apprehending thorny questions from various methodological perspectives. The event is an opportunity to discuss how to achieve criticality in qualitative research, beyond dominant and oppressive historical narratives.

Digital Independence and You

Digital Independence A workshop to empower YOU, the computer user! Are you a computer user? Do you feel powerless towards your computer sometimes? Have you experienced being afraid of Windows updating during an exam? Or having to buy a new computer because Windows or macOS does not support your old computer anymore, even though it works perfectly fine? Do you want to become (a bit) independent of American Big Tech? Have you ever considered that you can change these tools to make them fit your needs, your device, and the world? If you are silently thinking, “Oh, but I am not a computer nerd”; I don’t know anything about computers, this is the workshop for you! The program: - basic terms and what an operating system does - the differences in major operating systems - open-source and free software - software use from a political perspective and sustainable software - how to change your operating system and try out the alternatives What to bring: We will try out stuff in the workshop, so if you have the possibility, bring a laptop and some empty storage media (for example, an USB stick: 4 GB is good, 8 GB is even better, and more is no problem :)). P. S.: If you are a computer nerd you are of course also welcome, but we will probably cover knowledge you already have :)
